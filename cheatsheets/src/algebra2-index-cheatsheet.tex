\cheatsheettitle{Algebra II Module Index}{Prerequisite Map}

\section*{How to Use This Index}
Use this as the organizing map for Algebra II reference sheets. Each module should stand alone, but later modules depend on fluency from earlier algebra and function work.

\section*{Module Sequence}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clp{3.55in}}
\toprule
Order & Module & Main Skills \\
\midrule
1 & Algebra Review & Equation solving, inequalities, factoring, radicals, rational exponents, function notation \\
2 & Functions and Transformations & Parent functions, transformations, composition, inverses, domain/range, piecewise functions \\
3 & Quadratics and Optimization & Forms of quadratics, discriminant, complex solutions, maximum/minimum applications \\
4 & Polynomial Functions & End behavior, multiplicity, division, rational roots, factor and remainder theorems \\
5 & Rational Functions & Domains, holes, asymptotes, intercepts, graphing, rational equations \\
6 & Radicals and Rational Exponents & Rewriting forms, simplifying, radical equations, extraneous solutions \\
7 & Exponential and Logarithmic Functions & Exponent rules, log rules, change of base, equations, growth and decay \\
8 & Sequences and Series & Arithmetic/geometric sequences, finite sums, infinite series, sigma notation \\
9 & Trigonometry & Unit circle, exact values, trig graphs, identities, inverse trig, trig equations \\
10 & Triangle Trig & Right-triangle trig, Law of Sines, Law of Cosines, area, ambiguous case \\
11 & Conic Sections & Circles, parabolas, ellipses, hyperbolas, completing the square \\
12 & Complex Numbers and Polar & Complex arithmetic, polar coordinates, De Moivre, roots \\
13 & Counting, Probability, and Statistics & Permutations, combinations, conditional probability, independence, z-scores \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section*{Common Course Habits}
\begin{itemize}
  \item State restrictions before solving rational, radical, and logarithmic equations.
  \item Use exact values unless a decimal approximation is requested.
  \item Connect formulas to graph features: intercepts, domain, range, asymptotes, and end behavior.
  \item Check for extraneous solutions after squaring, clearing denominators, or using logarithms.
\end{itemize}
